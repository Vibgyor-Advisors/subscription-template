INTRODUCTION

Africa, being a large continent is prone to diverse issues and problems like high prevalence of communicable diseases, low incomes, malnutrition, poorly staffed services, low life expectancy and the like. The most crucial amongst such problems is mental health issues of children which remains unaddressed to a very large extent. 

One of The major causes of growing psychological problems in the continent are increasing poverty, wars, natural disasters and other such sorts of violence and social unrest. It has been revealed that the element of depression alone is going to be the second biggest burden of all health conditions by 2030

In Africa, a plethora of mental health problems exist among children consisting of half of the population of the region. It is estimated that of the children aged between  0 to 9 years, about 3 % suffer from mental disorders. Many suffer from poor psychological development because of neglect either by their mothers or other caretakers. 

MENTAL HEALTH ISSUES OF CHILDREN

Child and adolescent mental health is defined by the WHO as the capacity to achieve and maintain optimal psychological functioning and well being. It is directly related to the competence achieved and level reached in psychological and social functioning.

It includes an idea of identity and self-worth, sound family and peer relations, an ability to learn and be productive and capacity to use cultural resources and developmental challenges to maximize development.

However, if there are patterns or changes in thinking, behaving or feeling that causes distress or disrupts a person’s ability to function it implies mental illness or mental health disorder. The  mental health disorder includes problems like disruptions or delays in developing age, inappropriate thinking, deviant behaviors, unregulated emotions or social skills which distress children and thereby disrupt their ability to function well in life.

In many African countries, the most usual presentation of psychosis is acute transient psychosis, paranoid psychosis and psychosis resulting from cerebral involvement in infectious diseases which produce temporary disability but may prove to be fatal if not treated properly.

The children growing up in such regions  are usually exposed to extremely crucial experiences, who became hardly compatible with physical as well as emotional survival. Unless these toxic environments are addressed by the systems of care, improvement in the mental health of the children is a goal unachievable.

 There are some human resources dedicated to the mental health of children. The pushes of the World Health Organization to include mental health in the millennium development goals have improved the profile of child mental health to a very large extent. However, there are certain challenges to the development of appropriate comprehensive child mental health services in Africa. The primary challenges say for instance are socio-economic circumstances and demographic complexities.

In Africa,both a formal as well as informal system of mental health care for children. Though the formal systems of care have been largely eroded by war or poverty but the informal system of care which includes care by family, by indigenous healers or NGO’s are to the large extent developed. For instance, the NGO’S like The Ape Action Africa, the Medical and Research Foundation (AMREF), the Etijah and the like launch certain programs that provide a great help in addressing the issues of orphans and vulnerable and thereby come to the rescue of poor and mentally ill children. 

However, for proper care of needy children, both care systems are important and both must work together and complement each other as both are interdependent, for a formal care system can’t effectively address the issue having social context without consulting the informal system of care. Similarly the informal system of care depends on a formal system for specialized training and supervision.

CURRENT SITUATION IN AFRICA

In Africa, mental health problems have assumed greater attention. The continent’s population grew by 49% between 2000 to 2015  and is expected to double by next three decades. There is constant pressure on young people who struggle to earn a livelihood in highly competitive labor markets. It is believed that they may face psychological problems because of their failure to realize ambitions or may turn to substance misuse for alleviating their frustrations.

The member states of the world health assembly in 2013, committed to the Mental Health Action Plan, whereby they pledged to enhance service coverage for severe mental health disorders by 20% by 2020 and to reduce suicide rates by 10%.

But the fact remains that health in general is still a poorly funded area of social services in most African countries, morality happening mostly by infectious diseases and malnutrition and morbidity and disablement due to mental illness receive little attention from the government. Mental services in particular continue to be poorly developed with no mental policies, programs or action plans.

RELIEF PROGRAMS & GOVERNMENT INITIATIVES

The member states of the African region of the world health organization adopted two resolutions in 1988 and 1990 to improve mental health services with the main aim to formulate mental health policies, programs and action plans.

Two years later, a survey was conducted to see if the countries adopting the resolutions have done anything in this regard. Though there were some modest achievements yet the situation of mental health programs in most of the countries including Africa was unsatisfactory.

WHO in its African Regional Strategy for Mental Health in the year 2020, emphasized that populations in the African region are occupied by various mental and neurological disorders happening to be the major cause of disability. To address this issue, WHO is undertaking a large number of  welfare projects with the aim to promote mental health services in Africa by strengthening mental health policies and implementing regional strategies to prompt mental health and thereby prevent mental, psychological and neurological disorders. In the long run, WHO intends to reduce disability associated with mental, psychological and neurological disorders through community based rehabilitation.

HOW TO SOCIALLY UPLIFT THE AFFECTED?

Mental disorders are counted among the top ten causes of disability in Africa the burden of which on health issues is going to rise making it inevitable to give this issue the attention and resources it needs.

While planning the system of child mental health care in Africa, two important factors that need to be taken into account include firstly, the moral value system  and cultural specific world views of Africa, Asia, Arab and Caucasian people and secondly the social context in which children grow.

It is the need of the hour that authorities in power should develop psychiatry and mental health care. The mental health policies should be strengthened and regional strategies should be adopted and implemented to prompt mental health and mental, neurological and psychological disorders. The drug abuse related problems should be prevented. Community based rehabilitation is required to be launched for reducing disability associated with neurological, mental and psychoactive substances.

People’s perceptions are usually influenced by traditional beliefs in supernatural causes and remedies which leads to unhelpful or health damaging responses to mental illness and stigmatization of mentally ill persons. The negative perception of people as well as policy makers regarding mental and neurological disorders should be changed through various awareness programs and by formulating or reviewing existing legislation in support of mental health. 

Equitable access should be provided to cost-effective mental, neurological and psychosocial care.

CONCLUSION

In order to improve the deteriorating situation of mental health services in Africa, there is dire for authorities to lay focus on the system of health care available for patients in general and children in particular. It should be the priority of the policy makers and authorities in power to make inpatient psychiatric services available at least for children suffering from psychiatric disorders.

Like other countries, the primary health care model has been adopted in Africa long ago but the mental health services were made vertical recently which however is largely based in mental hospitals, catering mainly for adults. Secondary and tertiary outpatient services for children have been started in some areas but only South Africa and Senegal have made inpatient Psychiatric services available for children.

when there is lack of general psychiatric services for children, Other sectors of medical care systems have correspondingly a greater responsibility in meeting mental health needs of patients in Africa and until the general psychiatric services are made available, other sectors are morally under the obligation to step forward and contribute to the humanitarian cause of coming to the rescue of helpless and devastated children. Disciplines like maternal and child health departments, pediatrics, schools, social welfare services, juvenile justice systems and prisons though general, fairly basic and non-specialized but are the obvious ones that have to play a role in contributing to the noble cause of maintaining the mental stability of children.

Oceans make the most of the earth’s hydrosphere and 72 percent of the earth’s surface. It is a precious asset; a great source of biodiversity, a great contributor to social well-being, economic prosperity, and quality of life. 

To govern the oceans and to ensure that maritime activities do not contaminate this resource, the United Nations through its several programs is trying to protect these. Also, the international law of the sea includes all kinds of legal frameworks regarding the sea and its applicability to inter-state relations.

FREEDOM OF THE SEA

Even before any law regarding seas came into being, Spanish and Portuguese claimed universal maritime dominion. However, the freedom of the sea doctrine came as a challenge to it. Hugo Grotius, a Dutch jurist, and philosopher was the propounder of the principle of the freedom of the seas (mare liberum). He defined the seas as being, like the air, limitless and therefore common to all. So, seas were declared to be free and belonging to none. This doctrine was strongly pressed by the great maritime and commercial powers. Besides, it remains one of the most fundamental concepts of the law of the sea and is now recognized to include freedom of fishing, laying of submarine cables and pipelines, navigation, and overflight of aircraft.

However, this notion limited the national rights and jurisdiction over the oceans surrounding a nation’s coastline. Subsequently, by the second half of the 20th century, serious conflicts arose as coastal states started to demand custom zones for exclusive fishing rights and for the conservation of maritime resources found in their continental shelves.

UNITED NATIONS CONVENTIONS ON LAW OF SEA

The United Nations International Law Commission, upon the request of the International Community, began codifying the laws related to oceans in 1949 and prepared four draft conventions which were adopted by the United Nations at its first conference on the law of the sea.

THE FIRST UNITED NATIONS CONFERENCE ON THE LAW OF THE SEA ( UNCLOS I )

The United Nations in 1958 convened its first conference on the law of the sea which was attended by eighty-two states. UNCLOS 1 adopted four conventions :

  1. The Convention on the Territorial Sea and Contiguous Zone,
  2. The Convention on the High Seas,
  3. The Convention on  Fishing and Conservation of the Living Resources of the High Seas, and
  4. The Convention on the Continental Shelf. 

Although it was a positive step towards the development of the law of the sea still, there were many loopholes in the convention like the breadth of the territorial sea belonging to the states was not decided under it.

THE SECOND UNITED NATIONS CONFERENCE ON THE LAW OF THE SEA (UNCLOS II)

This conference which was held in 1960 did not result in any international agreements as it once again failed to fix a uniform breadth of the territorial sea or arrive at a consensus on sovereign fishing rights.

THE THIRD UNITED NATIONS CONFERENCE ON THE LAW OF THE SEA (UNCLOS III)

Finally, in the 11th session, a conference was held in 1982 to deal with different drawbacks of the previous conventions. It has resolved many important issues pertaining to ocean usage and sovereignty such as:

  • It provided definition and breadth of different maritime zones; 
  1. Territorial Sea:

It is a portion of the sea which is adjacent to the coast and over which the coastal states can exercise sovereignty subject to the right of foreign ships to innocent passage. Under UNCLOS III the territorial water limit was agreed to be 12 nautical miles measured from the baseline.

  1. Exclusive Economic Zone: 

It is an area beyond and adjacent to the territorial sea extending up to 200 nautical miles seaward from the baseline. In this zone, the coastal states have exclusive rights and duties, to, amongst other things, explore, exploit, conserve and manage the living, nonliving, and natural resources within this area. 

  1. Continental Shelf:

It is that area of the coastal state which comprises the sea-bed and subsoil of the submarine areas that extend beyond its territorial sea up to a distance of 200-350 nautical miles from the baseline. However, it shall not exceed beyond that.

  • It provided certain rights to Coastal states as well as other states:  
  1. Rights of Coastal States: 

They have certain exclusive rights including the right of fishing, rights over sea bed resources, rights to enact laws and regulations relating to transport and navigation, and the right to hold states responsible for non-compliance, etc.

  1. Right of other states:

The convention secures the “right of innocent passage” to other states through the territorial water of coastal states. Such a right implies that as long as a vessel has a purpose of traveling across the territorial sea, it amounts to innocent passage, which cannot be hindered by the coastal state.  

  • It provided for the establishment of International Seabed Authority; 

It has also established an inter-governmental body known as the International Seabed Authority to organize, regulate, and control activities that are mineral-related in the international sea-bed area beyond the limits of the continental shelf of coastal states. 

  • It provided conflict resolution methods; 

For three decades, dispute settlement under this convention has flourished, in the form of negotiation, mediation, conciliation, arbitration, judicial settlement, and formation of the commission on the continental shelf.

Protection of Marine Environment and Biodiversity

Oceans, despite being essential for survival, are subjected to a lot of degradation due to destructive human activities such as resource extraction, overfishing, pollution, etc. The United Nations under its various programs is working towards the protection of Marine Environment and Biodiversity:

The United Nation Environmental Programme (UNEP) is trying to work out solutions and strategies which will diminish human impacts and promote good use of marine and coastal ecosystems:

  1. It is working to promote and restore coastal blue carbon ecosystems by advancing science-based management approaches, international cooperation, a regional and global assessment by using practical methods to conserve and restore this ecosystem. 
  2. It is also assisting nations to adopt equitable and effective uses of the marine environment while protecting areas by furnishing technical support and building a capacity for the management of these areas. 
  3. World’s coral reefs, which generate millions of dollars annually, are degrading at a high rate due to destructive fishing, unsustainable development, ocean acidification, etc. UNEP is trying to protect these reefs by working with different stakeholders and partners such as International Coral Reef Initiative (ICRI), Regional Seas Programme, Reef world, etc.  
  4. Since the coming of the Regional Seas Programme, the United Nations has significantly been able to address the generation of the world’s oceans through a “shared seas” approach, which is to engage adjacent nations in extensive and particular actions to conserve their common marine environment.
  5. UNEP is also hosting the Global Programme of Action (GPA)  for the protection of the marine environment from land-based activities to deal with land-based pollution which contributes to nearly 80% of marine pollution. It emphasizes addressing three primary source categories that are, marine litter, nutrient management, and wastewater. source: www.unenvironment.org

International maritime organization (IMO)

It was established as a specialized agency for the advancement of international maritime law. Its main purpose is to create and maintain a broad regulatory framework for shipping and safety, legal matters, environmental concerns, technical co-operations, and efficiency of shipping.

  1. Marine shipping and pollution: In order to reduce air pollution due to shipping, IMO has adopted different measures. These include;
  • International Convention for the Prevention of Pollution from Ships, 1973, now known as MARPOL. It addresses pollution from ships caused due to noxious liquid substances, harmful substances, sewage, garbage, and oil. MARPOL has significantly been able to reduce pollution from international shipping. These measures have ensured that oil tankers are made and operated safely and in such a way so as to ensure very little oil is spilled in accidents.
  • With regard to chemicals, it has been instructed that the design of ships should be such as to reduce the risks to ships, their crew, and the environment. Moreover, it has been prohibited to discharge sewage into the sea.
  • It limits the use of main air pollutants contained in ships such as sulfur oxide and nitrous oxide. The emission of ozone-depleting substances has also been prohibited.
  • Additionally, a separate convention has been made known as the International Convention For The Prevention Of Pollution Of The Sea By Oil (oil pool).
  1. Polar Code: It is foreseen that the polar maritime traffic is going to increase in the coming years. These regions are visited because of commercial imperatives and growing tourism, in the north and south respectively. However, this should not hamper the life of the sea and the sustainability of the environment of Polar Regions. Moreover, the protection of the environment and security of ships navigating in tough, remote, and exposed Polar Regions has always been a concern for IMO. There are different challenges to the ships operating, that are a shortage of communication systems, good charts, different navigational aids, and poor weather conditions. 
  • With the growing polar shipping, IMO has addressed International concern about the protection of the polar environment and the safety of seafarers and passengers by introducing new regulations that all ships operating in these waters must comply with. 
  • International Code For Ships Operating In Polar Waters (polar code) is mandatory under both the International Convention For The Safety Of Life At Sea (SOLACE) and MARPOL. It sets out mandatory standards that cover the wide range of design and construction, equipment, operational and training concern, rescue, and the protection of the pristine environment and ecosystems of both the poles as well. 
  • Each chapter contains different aims and functions that include watertight and weathertight integrity, ship structure, machinery installations, the safety of navigation, voyage planning, training, etc. on one hand and prevention of pollution due to oil, chemicals, sewage, and the discharge of garbage on the other and.
  1. Piracy: An unauthorized act of violence committed by a private vessel in seas against another vessel with intent to loot or rob is called piracy. It is considered to be an international crime and pirates as enemies of every state.
  • At the beginning of the present century, the acts of piracy have increased particularly on the coasts of Somalia. At least, Eighty-four attacks and millions of dollars have been robbed, many have been killed, injured and hundreds of hostages were taken in a single year. To tackle this problem, representatives of Somalia urged the president of the Security Council for the safety of the shipping and navigation. Consequently, a resolution was adopted to condemn all acts of piracy in territorial waters and high seas in Somalia. 
  • Also, IMO has developed certain regulations and guidance through the Maritime Safety Committee (MSC) to secure and manage travel and trade across seas. Thus, it has addressed the issue of piracy and armed robbery against ships too. 
  • Due to the above-mentioned situation in Somalia, IMO is trying to enhance maritime security in west and central Africa, with the region’s maritime agreements. Fortunately, ill effects of piracy have been mitigated over time because of the adoption of several anticipatory measures by IMO with the support of shipping industries. Its armed robbery and piracy module has information publicly available with the globally integrated shipping information system.
  • Further,  the member states who seek to enlarge their national measures for the threat of piracy and different unlawful activities can get IMO’S assistance. 

Intergovernmental Oceanographic Commission

The United Nations educational, scientific, and cultural organizations’ Intergovernmental Oceanographic Commission (IOC)’ works to promote international cooperation and coordinates programs pertaining to marine research, services, hazard mitigation, observation systems, and effective management of the resources of coastal areas and oceans. This commission aims to foster management, administration, decision-making processes for sustainable development of the marine environment.

CONCLUSION

The United Nations through the conventions and formation of IMO covers various aspects of the sea such as navigation, fishing, deep seabed mining, etc. It is accepted today worldwide that the convention of 1982 is a ‘constitution for the oceans’, within which every activity pertaining to seas must be done. As far as resource extraction and utilization of these oceans in its different aspects are concerned, the same should be carried out in a way so as to not abate the marine environment and biodiversity.

 The U.S relations with Mexico could be regarded as indispensable because of the fact that the countries share land and maritime border in North America. Several treaties have been concluded between the two nations such as the Gadsden Purchase (an agreement between the U.S and Mexico, in which the U.S agreed to pay Mexico $10million for a 2, 09,670 square mile portion of Mexico) and the North American free-trade agreement (NAFTA) (an agreement signed by Canada, Mexico and U.S, creating a trilateral trade bloc in North America). Both are members of the various international organizations including the organization of the American States and the United Nations.

Despite the fact that both countries have close diplomatic, economic, and socio-cultural relations, illegal immigration and illegal trade in drugs and firearms continue to be the causes of differences between the two countries.

Origin

The first border dispute between The United States and Mexico arose because of the reason for migration. In an effort to control the inflow of English-speaking settlers, Mexico banned immigration to Texas from The United States. With the independence of Texas from Mexico in 1845, it became a part of The United States as a slave state by the efforts of the then Mexican President Antonio Lopez de Santa Ana. As a result of the independence of Texas, Mexico ended up its diplomatic relations with the United States.

Furthermore, in a fix to make the Rio Grande River the border between The United States and Mexico which would make Texas part of The United States, the then U.S President James Polk in 1845 offered to purchase California and New Mexico from the Mexican government which in turn was refused. As a mark of retaliation to the refusal, Polk sent military forces to the Rio Grande, and a full-scale US invasion followed with the seizure of Mexico City.

Thereafter, the peace treaty of Guadalupe Hidalgo was signed ending the Mexican-American war which obligated Mexico to relinquish present-day Arizona, California, New Mexico, parts of Colorado, and Nevada. In return for the war-related damage to the Mexican land, $15 million in compensation was paid by The U.S. In addition to this, the peace treaty also provided for the protection of the property and civil rights of the roughly 80,000 Mexican nationals living in The U.S.

In 1853, the then U.S President Franklin Pierce planned to construct a railroad to the Pacific ocean for which he purchased 30,000 square miles of land along the Mesilla Valley which runs from California to El Paso for $10 million. The Gadsden Purchase marks the last adjustment to the border between Mexico and The United States and also the resolution for an indispensable border dispute between the two.          

 Cross Border Issues

The U.S- Mexican relations have usually remained friendly, nevertheless, periodic tensions have emerged over some major as well as minor issues including trade disputes, immigration, border security issues, drug trafficking, border environmental issues, and water resource issues respectively.

Drug Trafficking 

Drug trafficking is a global illicit trade involving the cultivation, manufacture, distribution, and sale of substances that are subject to drug prohibition laws.

Mexico plays a major role in the US drug control policy as it is the primary source of and transit country for illicit drugs like heroin, marijuana, and synthetic drugs such as methamphetamine, fentanyl. Besides, Mexico is also a major transit country for cocaine produced in the Andean region of South America.           

According to the Drug Enforcement administration (it is an agency tasked with combating drug trafficking and distribution within the United States) 2019 National Drug Threat Assessment, Mexican drug trafficking organisations which are involved in drug trafficking, money laundering, and other violent crime continue to pose a greatest criminal threat to the United States.

In 1969, an aggressive search and seizure counternarcotics operation was launched on the U.S Mexico border, by the then U.S President Richard Nixon while declaring “war on drugs”. The government of the United States deployed the agents for the purpose of inspecting all the persons and vehicles crossing into The United States through the border.  

The United States created the Drug Enforcement Administration, DEA and there was some counternarcotics cooperation in the 1970s and 80s. However, the killing of one of its DEA agents in Mexico sparked outrage in The United States which led them to pursue the unilateral strategy to fight the war on drugs.

Data on drug seizures at the U.S. border indicate an alarming volume of trafficking taking place in recent years. Since 2009, heroin seizures at the southwestern border have almost tripled, while meth seizures quintupled. However, cocaine and marijuana remain two of the most commonly seized drugs along the southern borders, equating to millions of pounds seized by the U.S. Border Patrol.

Since 2012, the number of traffickers apprehended at U.S. borders has steadily increased from 364,768 to nearly 500,000 in 2014. From 2012 to 2015 the U.S. Border Patrol has seized more than:

8.2 million lbs. of marijuana

32,600 lbs. of cocaine

34,000 ounces of heroin

17,600 lbs. of methamphetamine

Unfortunately, despite the high volume of search & seizure programs that have effectively stopped millions of pounds of illicit drugs from entering the  U.S,  drugs are still finding their way across and have a profound impact in many states along southern and coastal borders.

Unauthorised Immigration 

Unauthorized immigration refers to the migration of people into a country in violation of the immigration laws of that country or the continued residence of people without the legal right to live in that country.

Unauthorized immigration has always been a subject of greater concern between The US and Mexico with much emphasis on how to prevent unauthorized migration and address the large population of illegal migrants living in the United States. Mexico being the largest source of unauthorized migrants in The United States, the U.S migration policies including stepped-up border and interior enforcement have primarily affected Mexicans as more Mexicans have been leaving the United States than arriving. However, the Mexicans living in the United States, including those who are unauthorized remain the top priority of the Mexican government. 

Undocumented Guatemalans carry their belongings after crossing the Guatemala-Mexico border

While the concern of the US government over unauthorized immigration was on peak in the 1990s, it came up with the strategy for tracking down the unauthorized immigrants living in the  United States including the enforcement plan increasing border security and granting power to local law enforcement in the Border States to uphold immigration laws.

Since the mid-2000, successive Mexican governments have supported efforts to enact the Immigration reform in the United States, while being careful not to appear infringing upon US authority to make and enforce immigration laws. Mexico has made efforts to combat transmigration by unauthorized migrants and worked with US law enforcement to combat alien smuggling and human trafficking. 

NAFTA and USMCA

In 1980, the then US President Ronal Reagan put forth the idea of the North American Free Trade Zone as a part of his presidential campaign. After signing the Canada-United States free trade agreement in 1988, the governments of the US, Mexico, and Canada agreed to negotiate on what became NAFTA but even after ratification of the agreement by their respective capitals, NAFTA faced significant opposition in both US and Canada. 

Eventually, all the three countries ratified NAFTA in 1993, and finally, it came into force a year later, after the addition of the two side agreements to it, The North American Agreement on Labor cooperation NAALC (it is the side agreement to NAFTA under which each of the three countries that are parties to NAFTA agreed to enforce their own labor standards and to strive to improve labor standards in their country) and The North American Agreement on Environmental Cooperation NAAEC (it is an environmental agreement between the US Canada and Mexico as a  side treaty to NAFTA which sets out objectives concerning the conservation and the protection of the environment as well as concrete measures to further cooperation on the matters between the three countries).

Effects of NAFTA:

• NAFTA eliminated barriers to trade and investment between the US, Canada, and Mexico.

• It eliminated most tariffs with the major focus on liberalizing trade in agriculture, textiles, and automobile manufacturing.

• The agreement also sought to protect intellectual property, establish dispute resolution mechanisms and through side agreements, implement labor and environmental safeguards.

• NAFTA fundamentally proved beneficial to North American economies and the average citizens but harmed a small minority of workers in industries exposed to trade competition.

On the other hand, critics of the agreement argue that it was to be blamed for job losses, wage stagnation driven by low wage competition, and a widening trade deficit.

After US President Donald Trump took office in 2017, all the three signatories a year later reached an agreement to modernize the 25-year-old NAFTA with the United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement (USMCA) and all the three countries had ratified it in 2020. The USMCA took effect on July 1, 2020, replacing NAFTA. The new United States- Mexico-Canada Agreement (USMCA) will support mutually beneficial trade leading to freer markets, fairer trade, and strong economic growth.

Cross Border Environmental Issues 

Environmental health and environmental quality issues along the US-Mexico border have always been a concern for several years. Transboundary and shared resources and conflict include the flow of raw sewage and industrial wastewater into the United States from Mexico which has caused health and environmental problems in the border region.

Wastewater collection and treatment system capacity has not kept pace with rapid population growth in the border region. Also, the aging of existing wastewater infrastructure has resulted in increased maintenance issues such as pipeline ruptures. To address border sanitation issues, International Boundary and Water Commission (IBWC), The US Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), and the North American Development Bank (NADB) have been engaged to construct wastewater infrastructure on both sides of the border.

Recent Development and Crisis

Since the beginning of Donald Trump’s Presidential Campaign, U.S-Mexico relations have been in crisis. The bilateral relationships between the two countries have been seriously affected by Trump’s criticism of Mexico and its people, his threats to deport millions of undocumented Mexicans, his desire to build what he calls a “beautiful wall” on the southern border, and his express intention to abandon NAFTA. For Mexico officials and intellectuals, the current state of affairs has reached the lowest point in the two countries’ relationship over the last 25 years.

President Trump refocused the Merida initiative through executive orders on combating transnational criminal organizations and enhancing border security. Though Trump has praised Mexico’s enhanced efforts to reduce migration flows but criticized Mexico’s anti-drug performance. On the proposal of the Mexican government in 2019, a high-level security working group, with a view to combating organized crime and cross-border crime, was agreed to be created by the U.S government which includes the Merida initiative as one aspect of bilateral efforts.

          Trump’s Border Wall

Throughout Donald Trump’s 2016 election campaign, he called for the construction of a bigger, more impenetrable border wall with a view to preventing illegal immigration from Mexico along the U.S-Mexico border. In furtherance of this proposal, The Trump administration two years later announced new rules to deny asylum to anyone crossing the United States illegally. However, Mexico has substantially opposed the construction of the border wall as well as the Trump administration’s immigration policy for the reason that Mexico places high importance on immigration, not only because millions of co-nationals live in the U.S but also because they send millions of dollars to the country.

Conclusion 

For the first time over the last 25 years, the U.S-Mexico relations have withered away. The structural system that has prevailed for more than two decades is now under considerable threat. Although USMCA succeeded in the 25 years old NAFTA its future at present remains unpredictable. The nature of the bilateral relationship is likely to change substantially if the U.S implements its immigration reform which will have an enduring impact on the economies of both countries.

The U.S government’s immigration reforms and the modification in NAFTA have adversely affected Mexican nationalism and the perception of the U.S. Thus, the consequence of Trump’s campaign and his presidency has lead to the collapse of the bilateral relations between the two countries on one hand and strengthened the unity of Mexico’s citizens against the US Foreign policy on the other hand.

Data privacy is of greater importance in a world with ever-growing mountains of big data. Digitization of Services

Data Privacy

Primarily, privacy is the Right of a citizen of being free from public scrutiny or from having one’s personal information shared. Data Privacy is the right of the citizen to have control over how one’s personal information is collected and used. It deals with the practices which ensure that the data so shared by any person must be used for its intended purpose.

Water Scarcity: A Global Crisis

Physical water scarcity or Absolute water scarcity refers to a situation when there isn’t an adequate amount of water available to meet the demand, i.e. demand outpaces the limited water resources available in a given region. 

Economic water scarcity refers to water scarcity which occurs primarily because of poor infrastructure or mismanagement of water resources. It may also arise due to unregulated use of water in agriculture or industry, at the expense of the general population.

Introduction

Healthcare is the maintenance or improvement of health via the prevention, diagnosis, treatment, recovery, or cure of disease, illness, injury, and other physical and mental impairments in people. Health care is delivered by health professionals in allied health fields. Physicians and physician associates are part of these health professionals. Dentistry, pharmacy, midwifery, nursing, medicine, optometry, audiology, psychology, occupational therapy, physical therapy, athletic training, and other health professions are all part of health care. It includes work done in providing primary care, secondary care, and tertiary care, as well as in public health.

Types of Healthcare:

  1. Primary care
  2. Secondary care
  3. Tertiary care

1. Primary Healthcare

Primary care includes the first place patients go for medical care. It refers to the work of health professionals who act as the first point of consultation for all patients within the health care system.

Primary care team members include:

  • Primary care doctors also called general practice, general internists, or family doctors
  • Pediatricians (doctors for babies and children)
  • Nurses
  • Physician assistants
  • Nurse practitioners

2. Secondary Healthcare 

Secondary care includes acute care: necessary treatment for a short period of time for a brief but serious illness, injury, or other health condition. This care is often found in a hospital emergency department. Secondary care also includes skilled attendance during childbirth, intensive care, and medical imaging services.

The term “secondary care” is sometimes used synonymously with “hospital care”. However, many secondary care providers, such as psychiatrists, clinical psychologists, occupational therapists, most dental specialties or physiotherapists, do not necessarily work in hospitals.

3. Tertiary Healthcare

This type of healthcare is known as specialized consultative healthcare usually for inpatients and on referral from primary and secondary healthcare for advanced medical investigation and treatment. The following examples of tertiary care services are plastic surgery, burn treatment, cardiac surgery, cancer management, neurosurgery, complex medical and surgical interventions, etc.

Health System

A health system is an organization of people, institutions, and resources that deliver health care services to meet the health needs of target populations.

Key Components of a Well Functioning Health System 

As per WHO a well functioning health system responds in a balanced way to a population’s needs and expectations by: 

• Improving the health status of individuals, families and communities. 

• Defending the population against what threatens its health.

• Protecting people against the financial consequences of ill-health. 

• Providing equitable access to people-centered care.

• Making it possible for people to participate in decisions affecting their health and health system.

Thus it also includes the healthcare facilities being affordable to people despite their financial conditions.

What Does Affordability Mean?

Although the “affordability” of healthcare is a common concern, the term is rarely defined. Affordability is fundamentally a function of income, spending, and judgments about the value of goods and services for their price. Affordability is mostly taken as an economic concept.

Much of today’s health care focus centers on the rising costs of healthcare. Today’s challenging economic situation has redirected much of the health care discussion to affordability, not simply health care costs.

As a generic term, affordability can best be defined as a measure of someone’s ability to purchase a good or service. It describes whether a person or organization is able to make a purchase without unacceptable or unreasonable sacrifices, with limited sources. Similarly, taking simple definition Healthcare Affordability generally describes whether a person or organization has sufficient income to pay for or provide for health care costs. These costs could be insurance premiums or direct health care service costs.

Health care would be affordable if all the classes of society are able to pay for the delivery of the service or when the states spend a high economy on the health systems and provide it at low costs or free of costs or through insurance and fundings. 

Basis for Affordable Healthcare

1. Right to Health

The right to health is the economic, social, and cultural right to a universal minimum standard of health to which all individuals are entitled. The concept of a right to health has been enumerated in international agreements which include the Universal Declaration of Human Rights, International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights, and the Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities.

  • The Constitution of WHO defines the right to health as “the enjoyment of the highest attainable standard of health,” and enumerates some principles of this right as healthy child development; equitable dissemination of medical knowledge and its benefits; and government-provided social measures to ensure adequate health.

Characteristics of Capitalist Economic System

In a capitalist economic system, private individuals and/or businesses own the capital good, that is, production. The production of goods and services is wholly based on the supply and demand in the market. The private individuals are unrestrained and without any interference from the side of government. Some of the chief characteristics of a Capitalist Economy could be summarized as:

Producers are free to produce any goods or services as per demand in the market. Similarly, consumers are free to buy the things they desire. The driving force behind a capitalist economy is the profit motive. Business establishments exist to make a profit. Conclusion

Over the years, the capitalist economy has evolved. Starting with Feudalism followed by mercantilism and then capitalism. The capitalist economy needs a free market to succeed. Distribution of goods in a capitalist economic system is done according to laws of supply and demand.  In a capitalist economy, the best products are generated for the best prices because consumers will pay more for what they want the most. The essential reward of a capitalist economy is the innovation of new products and more efficient production methods. The essential feature of capitalism is the motive to make a profit. In a capitalist economy, there is rational self-interest that can lead to economic growth. However, like anything else, a capitalist economy has its disadvantages too. No doubt that the capitalist economy has surpassed economic growth more than that of other economic systems, but the concentration of wealth in the hands of few always remains controversial. The forces that lead to the success of a capitalist economy may also act as a reason for its failure; it is necessary to take appropriate measures to protect the free market from powerful private interests.
